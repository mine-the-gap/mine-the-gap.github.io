MINE-THE-GAP

Where, when, what, and how much is mined

Victor Maus & Huw Werner

Mining is in the spotlight due to the global energy transition, yet data gaps remain.

The Knowledge Gap

In our research, we geo-located several thousand mines worldwide. To our surprise, we found that more than half of mining impacts are undocumented globally.

The main reason for this gap is that we lack methods to measure mining impacts in a consistent way.

Source: Maus & Werner (2024) Nature

How can we produce timely, locally relevant, and globally reliable mine-level indicators?

Aim & Innovation

The goal of MINE-THE-GAP is to develop scalable models to map mining indicators using Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Satellite Earth Observation.

The Strategy: We combine multi-modal remote sensing data innovatively. We use high-resolution imagery to increase training data availability, and lower-resolution (but continuous global coverage) satellites to compose the predictor space for AI models. This allows us to produce reliable models that can be applied globally.

Outcomes

The project delivers four key sets of models and data:

1. Land Use

Geolocation of mining infrastructures, such as open pits and waste piles.

2. Volumes

3D models to measure volumes, helping estimate how much waste is generated.

3. Commodities

Detection of commodities to link mining impacts to their underlying economic drivers.

4. Integration

A validated framework to answer: Where, when, what, and how much is mined worldwide.

Outlook

The global energy transition and digitalization are increasing demand for minerals. MINE-THE-GAP addresses the urgent need for spatial information to understand these impacts.

Innovation

  • New set of indicators to measure mining impacts.
  • Scalable methods to map mine-level indicators globally.
  • Comprehensive mining data independent from company reports.

Impact

  • Methods to scale remote sensing applications.
  • Enabling research on mining and sustainability.
  • Increasing transparency in the mining sector.
  • Supporting the European Green Deal and SDGs.
